Portfolio

SENSEI

SENSEI (For Smart Estimation of Network StatE Information) is a distributed microservice infrastructure designed to passively detect, harvest, and share, the state of a communication network characterized by extreme heterogeneity of infrastructure, available resources, and data flows. I actively lead the project and maintain the backend services (GO, C/C++, JAVA).

MOCKETS

Mockets is a communication library designed to address challenges specific to mobile ad-hoc networks. Implemented at the application-level, Mockets offers several advanced functionalities to provide different classes of service, message prioritization/tagging, and overcome several limitations characteristic of the TCP protocol. I inherited and maintained the original C++ implementation of Mockets and developed a JAVA port in which I revamped the architecture.

GCPP

To support experimentation conducted by the NATO IST-124 Research Task Group on Heterogeneous Networks: Improving Connectivity and Network Efficiency, I lead the development of the Group Communication Protocol Platform (GCPP), a distributed test infrastructure designed to run on top of the ANGLOVA scenario to evaluate the performance of several pub/sub and analogous protocols in tactical networks. While working on this project, I gained familiarity with several solutions such as NATS, OpenDDS, TamTam, RabbitMQ, ZeroMQ, REDIS, DisService, MQTT, JGroups, and Edgeware Fabric.
